FAQ

Questions? Answers.

What types of businesses do you work with?

Growing UK businesses — particularly operational, service, construction, logistics and similar companies where valuable processes are still run through spreadsheets, email, repetitive admin or disconnected systems. If that sounds like your week, we can probably help.

Do I need a subscription to commission a project?

No. One-off projects are scoped and priced individually — see the starting points above. Plans exist for businesses that want ongoing guidance or consistent monthly delivery, and plan members get preferential access to project capacity.

Do you build custom software or use existing tools?

Both. We make an honest build-versus-buy call for every problem: custom-built where it matters, integrated with proven tools where that's the smarter option. The goal is the most effective, cost-efficient outcome — not the most billable one.

What counts against monthly delivery capacity?

Everything involved in shipping working systems: discovery, meetings, solution design, development, configuration, testing, documentation, deployment, revisions, troubleshooting and project communication. Capacity reserves your place each month and doesn't roll over — it's what keeps response times fast.

Are there any costs on top of the monthly price?

Third-party costs — AI model usage, automation platform subscriptions, hosting, databases, SMS or email delivery, and other external licences — are billed separately and always with your approval first. Larger builds may need a separate project scope, with your plan capacity counting towards the work.

How long does a typical project take?

Smaller workflow automations ship in a few weeks; larger software builds can take several months. Either way you get clear timelines upfront and regular demos throughout, so you're watching working software take shape — not waiting on a reveal.

Can you improve our existing systems and workflows?

Yes — most of our work enhances what you already have with better design, automation and integration. Full rebuilds only happen when they're genuinely justified.

What does the free strategy call involve?

Thirty minutes on your operations. We'll discuss your biggest operational bottleneck, identify the most promising areas for improvement and agree the most sensible next step — which is sometimes "don't build anything yet". No pitch deck, no pressure.
